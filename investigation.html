Protection


The purpose of the Investigation branch of Secret Service is to investigate counterfeiting, forging and changing currency, and securities, but it has expanded past that throughout the years. The investigation division has even had espionage under their during the Spanish-American War and World War 1, that authority diminished once the wars were over (Reese, 2013). Agents were given power to detect, arrest, and deliver anyone violating currency laws. They were also handed down investigation into anything against the “federal deposit Insurance Corporation, federal land banks, joint-stock land banks, and national farm loan associations” (Reese, 2013). Once computers came along it also handed down credit card and computer fraud. With new acts, the Secret Service could go after anyone making American currency outside the United States. Identity Theft and telemarketing scams were recognized and investigated (Reese, 2013). After 9/11, the Secret Service was given permission to “establish nationwide electronic crime task forces to assist law enforcement, private sector, and academic entities in detecting and suppressing computer-based crimes” (Reese, 2013). Sadly, investigation only takes 37% of Secret Services funding (Reese, 2013). Even with less founding the investigation part of the Secret Service is still amazing and very valuable part of the United States.
